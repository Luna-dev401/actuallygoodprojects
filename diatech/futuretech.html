What the Future Holds

The future is coming, and new developments in technology are shaping the world of energy and how we interact with our environment. In the future, technology will be our most powerful weapon in the fight against climate change, but to get there, we need researchers and funding to develop these new technologies.

Carbon Capture and Storage

Surprisingly, a way exists for coal and gas power plants to be almost carbon-free - CCS, or Carbon Capture and Storage. It works by separating CO2 from other gases when they are emitted, and transporting them via pipeline underground back into storage. There are several disadvantages to this, such as high costs, but this could be a way to keep using oil, natural gas and coal (which governments are apparently so insistent on doing) with less environmental impact. It's worth noting that using CCS still does produce emissions and has the opportunity to be disastrous if a pipeline ever fails.

Supercharged Solar Energy

With solar energy being the most viable renewable option for individual homeowners, it would be great if there was a way to make it even more efficient. In 2024, new photovoltaic cells manufactured with a special material called perovskite, far surpassing average energy thresholds of 15-20% with an energy efficiency of over 30%, while being surprisingly cheap to produce, at least with current material availability. They do, however, contain small amounts of lead, which does pose health concerns, but they are already being put into production and have already proven their effectiveness.

Green Hydrogen and Energy Storage

Green hydrogen, produced using renewable energy, offers a clean alternative for sectors like heavy industry and aviation. Despite its potential, it currently receives a small share of climate-tech venture funding. Additionally, innovations in battery storage are crucial for managing the intermittent nature of renewable energy sources like solar and wind. High-capacity, sustainable batteries enable consistent power supply.

Plasma Energy?

While we are probably still a few decades away from an alternative energy revolution, plasma energy is one of the most promising and boundless new technologies that is already in development. Chinese and Korean scientists have found a way to generate a steady loop of plasma which seems to feed itself, creating almost limitless energy if continuously remaining stable. In January 2025, they kept plasma stable inside an 'artificial sun' for over 1,000 seconds, demolishing the previous record of only about 2 seconds. This could signal a potentially enormous breakthrough in fusion energy generation.
