Climate Change is a Problem.

When greenhouse gas emissions are released into the atmosphere, they trap the suns heat which leads to climate change. Climate change is the gradual increase in the temperature of the earth, generally caused by the greenhouse affect, which is due to the increased levels of carbon dioxide and other pollutants. This rise in temperatures has the potential to get much worse in the future, so we must act now.

Why are there so many CO2 emissions?

A significant amount of the world's emissions are produced by burning fossil fuels like coal, oil, and natural gas to generate heat and power. Only about 30% of electricity is generated from renewable sources like wind and solar. The majority of electricity is still generated from fossil fuels. And nearly 90% of all carbon dioxide emissions are from fossil fuels. Manufacturing and industry produce emissions, mostly from burning fossil fuels to produce energy for making things like cement, iron, steel, electronics, plastics, clothes and other goods. Mining and other industrial processes also release gases.

Climate Change Causes Problems

From disrupted migration cycles, siginificant heat stress, increased precipitation, higher risk of droughts and heatwaves, rising sea levels due to melting sea ice, and a plethora of other problems, there is no doubt that climate change can have enormous negative impacts on our planet and its ecosystems. Our society has been designed to be powered by fossil fuels for the past several centuries - now, with the threat of climate change's impacts already coming on the horizon, we must adapt and ensure our homes and our lives are created, powered and continued sustainably.
