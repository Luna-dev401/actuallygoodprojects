The Solutions We Have

While it may be easy to lose hope, we already have plenty of solutions available to us. These include:

• Reduce usage of fossil fuels
• Invest in renewable energy
• Switch to sustainable transport
• Improve farming and encourage vegan diets
• Restore nature to absorb more carbon
• Protect forests like the Amazon

Renewable Energy

Renewable energy refers to various types of energy which can be infinitely renewed. Unlike coal, oil and natural gas, renewable energy will never run out, and almost all renewables are completely carbon-free, causing little to no harm to the environment. Renewable energy is becoming cheaper and cheaper, and with its low maintenance cost it has become even more money-efficient than non-renewable energies such as fossil fuels.

Sustainable Transport

Electric cars and public transport are already becoming far more popular across the world. The EU has promised to stop selling petrol cars by 2030, and in China, more than 50% of the cars sold last year were electric. Public transport such as buses and trains can also help a lot in reducing emission density, bringing many people to where they need to go with the emissions of only one vehicle.

Sustainable Diets

Vegan and vegetarian diets are far less harmful to the environment than meat-based diets. Animals produce methane, another harmful greenhouse gas which warms the planet, while plants take in carbon dioxide and reduce its concentration in the atmosphere. Many people around the world are switching to vegan and plant-based diets not just for the health benefits but also for a better environmental impact.

Restoration and Conservation

Regrowing destroyed forests and protecting endangered animals from the impacts of climate change can help preserve our priceless nature for the future. Climate change is a big threat for many species of animals, and deforestation and habitat loss just compound the threat. Conservation initiatives and reforestation are taking place all over the world, hopefully helping threatened species survive.
